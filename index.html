ABOUT ME.

I’m Brazilian, from the southern state of Santa Catarina, but my path has taken me to discover new worlds in Colombia, Germany, and, now, Italy. Also, the academy gives me the chance to meet new people, immerse myself in different cultures, and see the world from different perspectives. That curiosity and connection keep me moving forward, both in research and in life.

    • Perspectives for the Application of Reinforcement Learning for the Integrated Order-Dispatching and Maintenance Scheduling

      This study reviews the use of Reinforcement Learning for integrating production and maintenance scheduling. While traditional approaches rely on heuristics and mathematical programming, Industry 4.0 technologies now enable data-driven methods that open new possibilities. The findings position Reinforcement Learning as a promising path for advancing industrial scheduling practices in the era of Industry 4.0.

    • Advancing pharmaceutical waste management: An agent-based approach to enhance consumer participation in circular economy

      This study explores the application of circular economy principles in the pharmaceutical sector, focusing on consumer behavior and medication return systems. Using an Agent-Based Modeling approach, we simulate how consumers purchase, use, and dispose of medications to evaluate strategies such as consumer education, pharmacist training, and expanded collection infrastructure. It serves as a decision-support tool for policymakers, helping design targeted interventions to improve pharmaceutical waste management and advance circular economy goals.

    • Machine Learning Applied to Logistics Decision Making: Improvements to the Soybean Seed Classification Process

      This paper presents a method to optimize soybean seed classification in Brazilian agribusiness by predicting lead and queue times to guide resource allocation. The approach combines discrete event simulation with machine learning in a real-world setting. Data are collected to structure simulation scenarios, simulations generate artificial historical data, and machine learning predicts processing and waiting times. The method is adaptable to other logistics problems involving both human and equipment resources.

    • Adaptive Simulation-Based Optimization for Production Scheduling: A Comparative Study

      This study evaluates adaptive simulation-based optimization approaches for flow shop production planning. The methods combine genetic algorithms with discrete event simulation to account for system stochasticity and optimize production performance. Results show that the rule-free sequencing approach outperforms the dispatching-rule method.

    • Towards fashion renting: identification of influencing factors for consumer behavior

      This paper addresses sustainability challenges in the fashion industry by examining Fashion Renting as a strategy to extend product lifecycles and reduce environmental impact. Through a systematic literature review, the study aggregates and clarifies the factors influencing customer behavior in collaborative consumption. The resulting framework provides guidance for researchers to develop more accurate simulations of consumer behavior and for practitioners to design effective business strategies in the fashion sharing economy.
